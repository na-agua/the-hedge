hedge riding · the otherworld · spirit work

The hedge is not decoration.

It is the boundary between the world you can touch
and the world that touches you back.

To cross it is not imagination.
It is the oldest practice there is.

·

What hedge riding actually is

Hedge riding is the deliberate practice of entering an altered state of consciousness to journey into the Otherworld — the spirit realm beyond ordinary perception. It is not visualization, guided meditation, or imagination, though it begins there. It is a real crossing.

The hedge witch is the original walker between worlds — a figure who lives in ordinary life and can also move in the spirit realm. Not permanently in either. Belonging to both and to neither.

This practice sits at the center of hedge witchcraft. Everything else — herbs, moon work, shadow work — prepares you for this. Or it supports you after.

·
Before you cross

Prerequisites

I

Learn to be still

Before you can cross the hedge, you need to be able to sit in stillness for at least 20 minutes without significant distraction. This is not meditation mastery — it is the minimum threshold. The Otherworld does not open for a restless mind.

II

Know a banishing practice

The LBRP (from Kraig's Lesson 2) is ideal. Or any practiced, embodied protection ritual that you know in your body, not just your head. Do this before every single crossing. Not because the Otherworld is inherently dangerous, but because intention and protection determine what you encounter there.

III

Set a clear intention

Know why you are crossing before you cross. What are you seeking? Healing, knowledge, communication with an ancestor, understanding a dream? Curiosity alone is not enough. The Otherworld responds to purpose.

IV

Establish your return anchor

A physical object in your hand — a stone, a root, a piece of wood. This is what you hold onto. If the journey becomes disorienting, squeeze it. It is your tether to your body and this world. Never cross without it.

V

Do not ride when depleted

Not during mental health crisis, extreme emotional distress, illness, or after alcohol. The Otherworld requires that you be present and grounded enough to return. If you are not sure whether you are stable enough — you are not stable enough.

·
The crossing itself

How to Ride

1

Protect the space

Do the LBRP or your practiced banishing. State clearly: "Only my guides and allies may come through. Nothing harmful may approach." Say it aloud. Your voice in the physical world carries authority in the spirit world.

2

Enter trance

Drumming at 4 beats per second (use a recording), repetitive breath, or deep stillness. Some hedge witches use mugwort tea or incense. Let ordinary mind soften — not disappear, soften. You are not losing consciousness. You are shifting it.

3

Find your threshold

Visualize a specific boundary — a hedge, a forest edge, a cave mouth, a river, a doorway in fog. This is your crossing point. Know it completely. Return to the same threshold each time you ride — consistency builds the path.

4

Cross

Part the branches. Step through. Do not force what you see — receive it. The Otherworld will show you what is there. Your job is to be present and observant, not to control the experience.

5

Work with what comes

An animal, an ancestor, a figure, a landscape, a feeling. Ask your intention question. Listen without immediately interpreting. What you encounter may not make sense until after you return.

6

Return deliberately

When complete — or when you feel it is time — retrace your steps to your threshold. Step back through deliberately. Close it behind you. Thank any guides or allies who were present. Say your own name aloud when you return.

7

Ground completely

Eat something. Touch the earth. Press your feet flat on the floor. Write everything immediately — what you saw, felt, heard, sensed. It fades like dreams within minutes. The journal is the harvest.

Protection when riding

✦
Always do a banishing first. Every time, without exception. The LBRP takes 10 minutes and it matters.
✦
Hold your anchor object. A stone, root, or wood in your hand. This is your tether to the physical world.
✦
Set a time limit. 20-30 minutes for a working ride. Use a gentle alarm. Open-ended journeys become disorienting.
✦
Not everything that appears is what it seems. Test the spirits. Ask them to state their name and intention. Genuine guides do not resist this.
✦
If something feels wrong, leave. You can always leave. Say "I return to my body now" — say your own name — squeeze your anchor. You are always able to come back.
