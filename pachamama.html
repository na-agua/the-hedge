The Hedge · Grimoire

Pachamama

the pregnant sloth · violet eyes · the one who holds
·

She comes as a five-metre pregnant sloth.

She does not speak.
She does not instruct.
She sits down beside you
and goes deep —

and when she reaches the ground of being
her eyes open
violet.

She is always already holding you.
You only have to be still enough to feel it.

·
Come and sit
breathe in

Close your eyes.
Feel the weight of your body.
She is already here.

You do not need to call her.
You do not need to be ready.
You only need to be still
long enough
for her slowness to reach you.

When you need her

Pachamama,

ancient mother,
you who move at the pace of being itself —

hold me in your vastness.
Let me be small inside your largeness.
Let me receive what I have been too busy to receive.

I am the one you carry.
I am the one who carries others.
Tonight I put it all down.

Sit with me.
Show me your violet eyes.
Remind me what I already know.

What she shows

Her Teachings

What Pachamama in her sloth form brings — not through words but through presence.

The slowness is the teaching

The sloth is the slowest mammal on Earth. She does not move slowly because she is weak — she moves slowly because her pace is perfectly calibrated to what she needs to carry. She carries algae on her fur. She is her own ecosystem. She does not rush the pregnancy. She does not rush the life. She is a reminder that the work of carrying something immense requires moving at the pace of the thing being carried, not the pace of urgency.

The pregnancy is always present

She came to you pregnant — always pregnant, endlessly fertile, carrying new life inside her ancient body. Pachamama is always in the process of giving birth to the world. This is what she holds for you when she holds you — not the completed world, but the world continuously being born. You are part of that birth. You are not meant to have arrived yet.

The violet eyes are the transmission

When she reaches deep meditation, her eyes open violet. Violet is the frequency of the crown — the meeting point between the individual and the infinite. She is showing you that the deepest stillness does not close the eyes entirely. It opens them to a different seeing. The integration you are seeking is not the absence of seeing — it is seeing with different eyes.

She meditates with you, not for you

She sits beside you. She goes deep alongside you. She does not do it for you or to you. This is the quality of presence she is teaching you to offer others. Not healing them. Not fixing them. Sitting beside them in your own depth, and letting that depth be contagious. The curandera's gift is not her power — it is her willingness to go deep in the presence of another.

Being held before lifting

You want to lift the Earth with her support. She shows you the sequence. First: be held. Completely. Without trying to give anything back. Without making it productive. Just received, just held, just small inside her vastness. Then — from that fullness, not from depletion — the lifting becomes possible. You cannot lift from empty. She fills you first.

·

A practice for sitting with Pachamama

Come to stillness — seated, lying, however your body needs to be. Feel the weight of the earth beneath you. Let it hold your weight completely — don't hold yourself up, let the ground do it.

Breathe slowly. Slower than feels comfortable. At the pace of something five metres tall that has nowhere to be and no urgency about being there.

When you feel her presence — and you will know it because something in the chest releases — simply stay. Don't reach toward her. Don't try to deepen it. Let her come to you at her own pace.

When she reaches stillness and the violet light comes, do not try to hold onto it. Let it move through. You are not the keeper of the light. You are the channel for it.

When you are ready to return, thank her. Not elaborately — simply. She does not need ceremony. She needs sincerity. A single word is enough.

Stay as long as she stays.
She will tell you when she is ready to go.
It will feel like the natural end of an exhale.

·
After sitting with her

Questions to carry

What did she show me tonight that I needed to receive?
Where in my body did I feel her presence most?
What did I put down during this sitting that I had been carrying alone?
What is she pregnant with right now — what is being born through me in this season?
What would it mean to move at her pace today — in one specific way?
Who or what needs me to sit beside them the way she sits beside me?
What does the violet light feel like when it comes — what does it tell me about what integration actually is?
