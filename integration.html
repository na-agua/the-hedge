The Integration

bringing what you touched into your hands
·

You have been to places
that have no address in ordinary language.

You have touched the ground of being.
You have seen what is beneath the seeing.
You have been held by something
that has no name in any human tongue.

The work is not more knowing.
The work is landing.

Integration is not the explanation of the experience.
It is the slow, patient work of letting
what you touched above
move all the way down into your feet.

·
The foundation

What Integration Actually Is

For the advanced practitioner who has already had multiple openings across multiple traditions.

First principle

The experience is complete. The integration is the rest of your life.

The medicine or the practice opened a door. You walked through. Integration is not going back through that door — it is learning to live as someone who has been through it. There is no finish line. There is only: how much of what I touched can I embody today, in this ordinary moment, with this ordinary person in front of me?

Second principle

Understanding is not integration.

You can understand an experience completely — map it, name it, place it in a tradition, explain it to someone else — and still not have integrated it. Integration happens in the body, in relationship, in action. The measure is not how clearly you can describe what happened. The measure is how you treat the person in front of you on an ordinary Tuesday.

Third principle

The gap between vision and embodiment is not failure.

You have seen the light completely. You know what is possible. And then you return to a body with old patterns, old wounds, old fears — and the gap between what you saw and how you live can feel unbearable. This gap is not evidence that the experience wasn't real. It is evidence that you are human, and that embodiment is the most difficult work there is.

Fourth principle

What arose that was difficult is as important as what was beautiful.

The medicine shows you everything — not just the light. What surfaced that was painful, confronting, or dark is not a mistake or a bad trip. It is the shadow asking to be included in the integration. The Crone comes to Ayahuasca ceremonies. She is not there to destroy you. She is there to show you what needs to be seen.

Fifth principle

You cannot give what you have not yet landed.

The curandera's work — touching and opening hearts, being a vessel for healing — requires that what she carries has been metabolized into her body, not just held in her vision. Integration is not preparation for service. It is the service itself, done on yourself first.

·

Working with what has no language

Some experiences resist language completely. Not because they are vague, but because they are too precise — too specific to a frequency that words were not built to carry. The dissolution of self. The recognition of something that was always true. The contact with what is before form. Dimensions, lives, planets, beings, the ground of being itself.

When language fails, these carry the experience instead:

The body — where did you feel it? Locate it physically. A sensation, a warmth, a quality of presence. The body remembers what the mind cannot articulate.

Drawing, color, mark-making — not to make something beautiful. To make something true. Crayon, charcoal, whatever. Let the hand move without the mind directing.

Sound — a tone, a hum, a note that carries the frequency. Some experiences can be sung that cannot be spoken.

A single word or image as an anchor — not to explain the experience but to mark it. A word that points toward it from outside, like a cairn on a path. You will know the word when you find it.

Silence held consciously — sitting with what cannot be said, and choosing not to fill it with approximations. Sometimes the most integrous response to the unspeakable is to protect its silence.

The questions

Integration Journal

Not all questions are for all experiences. Find the ones that feel alive for this particular opening.

Immediately after — within 24 hours
What do I know now that I didn't know before? Not understood — know.
What in my body feels different? Where?
What was the most uncomfortable moment? What was it showing me?
What was I shown about myself that I have been avoiding?
If I could carry only one thing back from this experience, what would it be?
What needs to be written before it fades — even in fragments, even in broken language?
The first week — while it is still close
What has shifted in how I relate to my own body?
Where am I still resisting what I was shown?
What did I see about my relationships that I now need to address?
What pattern in my life did this experience illuminate most clearly?
What is the medicine asking me to change in my daily life?
What part of the experience am I already explaining away or minimizing?
What symbol, image, or being appeared that I am still sitting with?
The first month — the slow landing
Where is the experience still alive in my body?
What have I actually changed in my life as a result of this experience?
What was I shown about my vocation — what I am here to do?
Where am I still performing the insight rather than living it?
What does this experience ask of me in relation to others?
What old identity is this experience asking me to release?
Where does the gap between what I saw and how I live hurt most?
Long arc — months and years
How has this experience changed how I meet suffering — my own and others'?
What wisdom from this experience has become ordinary, lived, embodied?
What from this experience am I still carrying above my head rather than in my hands?
How has this opening changed my relationship with death?
What does the person I was shown I could become require of me today — not eventually, today?
How am I serving the living and the dying differently because of what I have touched?
For experiences beyond language — non-ordinary, transpersonal, cosmic
What did I touch that had always been true but that I had never directly seen?
What is the relationship between what I experienced and this specific body, this specific life?
If what I touched is real — and it is — what does that change about how I live today?
What do the other lives, dimensions, or beings I encountered have to do with this one?
What is the medicine asking me to bring back to Earth specifically?
How do I stay tethered to the ground of ordinary human life while carrying what I carry?
What is the difference between what I was shown and what I am projecting onto the experience?

For the one who carries this for others

You are not integrating for yourself alone. You are integrating so that what you carry becomes medicine in your hands — a quality of presence, a capacity for contact, an ability to be with what is without being swept away by it.

The curandera who has not integrated her own openings becomes a vessel that leaks. Not through malice but through unfinished business — the places where her own wound is still raw enough to be activated by the wounds of the people she is trying to serve.

The most important question for the one who carries this for others is not "what did I learn?" but "am I clean enough to be a clear channel today?"

This is not about being perfect. It is about knowing your own landscape well enough to know when you are in your own material and when you are in someone else's. Integration builds this discernment.

After this experience, where am I most raw? Where in my own wound am I most vulnerable to being activated?
What did this opening show me about my capacity to be with others in their darkness without fixing, rescuing, or projecting?
What quality of presence was I given in this experience that I want to cultivate and offer?
Where do I still need my own healing before I can truly serve in this way?
How do I stay in contact with the source — the light, the ground, the medicine — in the ordinary days between the extraordinary ones?

Grounding the experience

Practices for Integration

The body is the integration vessel. These are not substitutes for sitting with what arose — they are ways to help it land.

The first hour — do nothing

After the experience ends, before anything else: lie down, let the body be still, and do not reach for your phone, your notebook, or another person. Let the experience settle in the silence. What wants to be the first thing that arises on its own is important.

Write in fragments, not narrative

Don't try to tell the story in order. Write whatever images, words, or feelings are most alive — in fragments, out of sequence, without explanation. The narrative can come later. First, catch what is most true before the mind organizes it into something manageable.

The body scan — three days after

Three days after the experience, lie down and slowly move awareness through the entire body from feet to crown. Notice where the experience still lives as sensation. These locations are where the integration is still working. Place your hands there. Breathe into them. Stay.

Return to nature within 48 hours

Bare feet on earth, body in water, hands in soil, face in wind. The medicine came from the living world. After the experience, return to the living world. Trees, particularly, are excellent integration companions — their rootedness is medicine for the ungrounded.

The LBRP as integration practice

Kraig's Lesser Banishing Ritual of the Pentagram is not just protection — performed after a significant opening, it helps reseal the aura and reorient the self within ordinary space and time. Do it slowly, with full intention. Feel the directions. Feel the archangels. Let the ritual say: I am here, in this body, in this world.

One change in ordinary life

Within one week of the experience, make one concrete change in your ordinary daily life that reflects what you were shown. Not a dramatic gesture — one small, real, sustainable change. This is how you tell the medicine that you received what it gave.

The full moon integration ritual

On the first full moon after a significant experience, take what you are still carrying to the moon. Sit outside or by a window with moonlight. Ask: what from this experience is ready to be released? What is ready to become part of who I am? These are different things. Honor both.

Sitting with what you cannot explain

Some of what you experienced will never be fully integrated because it is not meant to be understood — it is meant to be carried. There are things the medicine gives you that are not for your mind. They are for your presence. The practice is to stop trying to resolve them and learn to carry them with grace.

The light you touched is real.
The distance between that light and this life is real.
The work of closing that distance —
slowly, imperfectly, with great patience —
that is the practice.

Not to escape the Earth.
To bring the light all the way down into it.
Into your hands.
Into your voice.
Into the eyes of the person in front of you.
