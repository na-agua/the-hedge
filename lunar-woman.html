Moon Cycle Types

These patterns shift throughout life. None is better than another.

🌑 White Moon Cycle

You bleed with the new moon and ovulate at the full moon. Traditionally associated with nurturing, receptivity, and fertility — biological or creative. Your inward time aligns with the moon's inward time. Deep rest at the dark of the moon, full creative power when the moon is full.

🌕 Red Moon Cycle

You bleed with the full moon and ovulate at the new moon. Historically associated with healers, wisdom-keepers, and those whose power flows outward toward others rather than toward biological creation. When the world is most awake, you turn most inward. A paradox that produces deep magic.

🌒 Pink Moon Cycle

You bleed at the waxing moon. A transitional cycle — you are in a period of movement, from inward spiritual focus toward outward creative expression or vice versa. A time of building and becoming.

🌘 Purple Moon Cycle

You bleed at the waning moon. Your most inward time aligns with the moon's releasing energy. Deep clearing, introspection, and spiritual preparation. The cycle of the witch who does her most profound work in the dark.

·

If you don't menstruate: You can still work with this framework. Use the lunar cycle itself as your guide — new moon as day 1 (menstruation phase), waxing crescent and first quarter as follicular, full moon as ovulation, waning as luteal. The archetypal seasons apply regardless.

If your cycle is irregular: Track how you actually feel day by day and notice the pattern over months. The inner seasons are real even when the calendar is unpredictable.
