The Wheel & the Hedge Witch

For the hedge witch, the Wheel is not a decorative framework. It is a map of when the veil is thinnest — and therefore when crossing is easiest, most potent, or most dangerous.

Beltane and Samhain are the two great threshold festivals. The veil is thinnest at both. Hedge riding done at Beltane tends toward the Fae, the wild, the erotic and creative energies. Riding done at Samhain moves toward the ancestors, the dead, and the deeper shadow.

The solstices and equinoxes are points of astronomical balance — moments when the world literally pauses at a threshold before turning. These are powerful times for any practice that works with liminality.

The cross-quarter days (Imbolc, Beltane, Lughnasadh, Samhain) are the points between — the moments of transition from one season to another. They are liminal by nature. The hedge witch feels them in the body before the calendar marks them.

You don't need elaborate ritual for every sabbat. Acknowledgment is enough. A candle. A few written words. A moment outside in the weather that belongs to that turning. The Wheel knows when you are paying attention.
