The Shadow

inner dark · what hides · integration
·

The shadow is not your enemy.

It is the part of you that was told to hide —
the anger, the grief, the desire, the power
that felt unsafe to show.

Shadow work is not about fixing yourself.
It is about seeing clearly.

·
What shadow work is

Honest looking. Sitting with discomfort without running from it.

Finding the gift locked inside the wound.

Witnessing yourself without bypassing what you see.

Returning again and again to what you'd rather not look at.

What it is not

Therapy or direct trauma healing — that requires a human.

Wallowing in pain or retelling your story endlessly.

A destination you arrive at and finish.

Something that should feel comfortable or resolved.

·
The work itself

The Process

I

Name the trigger

Be specific. Not 'I feel bad' but 'when she didn't respond, I felt invisible and worthless.' The more specific you are, the more useful the work. Vague shadow work produces vague insight.

II

Sit with it in your body

Don't fix, analyze, or perform. Where does this feeling live? Chest? Throat? Belly? Stay there for five minutes without moving away. The body holds what the mind refuses.

III

Find the origin

Shadow wounds are usually old. The current trigger is almost never the origin. When did you first feel exactly this? How old were you? What was happening? The present situation is borrowing its power from the past.

IV

Find the hidden gift

Every shadow contains the seed of a strength. Shame about anger → anger is protection, healthy boundaries. Fear of abandonment → a deep capacity for love and loyalty. The shadow is not weakness dressed up — it is power that was never given a safe home.

V

The ritual act

Give it a physical form and release it. Write it and burn it. Bury it. Speak it under the waning moon and let the words dissolve into the dark. Don't journal about it after. Let it be done.

When the shadow is loudest

🌘
Waning moon, dark moon — the veil between you and your own unconscious thins. What was manageable becomes undeniable. This is not dysfunction, it is invitation.
🍂
Luteal phase (inner autumn) — Days 20–28 of your cycle. The Enchantress rises. What you've been tolerating all month becomes impossible to ignore. These strong feelings are your most honest self.
🕯️
Samhain season — October through early November. The veil between all worlds is thinnest. The shadow is never louder than when the ancestors are close.
🌑
3am — the liminal hour. When you wake at 3am with a feeling you can't name, that is your shadow asking to be seen. Keep a notebook beside the bed.

Going deeper

Journal Prompts

The mirror prompts

What emotion do I judge most harshly in others? (It lives in me.)
Who do I envy, and what does that envy tell me about what I secretly want?
What behavior in others makes me most angry? (It is asking me to look at myself.)

The origin prompts

What do I secretly believe about myself that I would never say aloud?
What did the child version of me need that she didn't receive?
What was I taught to suppress, hide, or be ashamed of?

The gift prompts

What would I do if no one was watching and I couldn't disappoint anyone?
What am I most afraid would happen if people saw me fully?
What part of myself have I been punishing, and what would it look like to stop?

The body prompts

Where in my body do I hold what I am not allowed to feel?
What does my body do when I am hiding something from myself?
What physical sensation lives just underneath the surface right now?

The Crone governs shadow work.

She is the third face of the Goddess —
after the Maiden's joy and the Mother's fullness
comes the Crone's ruthless clarity.

She does not comfort. She illuminates.
She strips away what is not real
so you can finally see what is.

When you do shadow work, you are sitting with the Crone.
Welcome her. She came because you are ready.
